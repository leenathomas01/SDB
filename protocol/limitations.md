# Limitations and Non-Goals

## Purpose

This document states the **explicit limitations** of this work and clarifies what it does **not** attempt to prove.

These boundaries are intentional.

---

## 1. Not a Deployment Specification

This repository does **not** provide:
- Production-ready protocols
- Performance-optimized implementations
- Security-hardened cryptographic systems

All designs are exploratory and illustrative.

---

## 2. No Claims About General Intelligence

The work does **not** claim:
- Emergent intelligence
- Consciousness
- Understanding
- Agency or autonomy

All behavior is the result of constrained, rule-bound simulation.

---

## 3. No Real-World Adversary Model

Adversarial testing was:
- Structural
- Localized
- Bounded

This work does **not** model:
- Nation-state attackers
- Side-channel attacks
- Real cryptographic threat models

---

## 4. Limited Scale

Experiments were conducted at:
- Small, fixed numbers of recipients
- Controlled role distributions

Scalability claims are theoretical extrapolations, not empirical guarantees.

---

## 5. Simplified Metrics

Metrics such as:
- Drift
- Clarity
- Entropy

are **abstract proxies**, not standardized measurements.

They are used comparatively within runs, not as absolute indicators.

---

## 6. No Learning or Adaptation Claims

Recursive anchoring:
- Does not modify models
- Does not change internal representations permanently
- Does not constitute learning

It is a bounded stabilization mechanism only.

---

## 7. No Human Factors Analysis

This work does **not** address:
- Usability
- Human interpretability beyond basic summaries
- Cognitive ergonomics

Narrative summaries are observational, not evaluative.

---

## 8. No Guarantee of Optimality

Passing all gates means:
- The protocol behaved as expected under tested conditions

It does **not** mean:
- The design is optimal
- The design is complete
- The design is universally applicable

---

## Summary

This repository demonstrates:
- What is possible under strict constraints
- What has been explicitly validated
- What has been consciously excluded

Any extension beyond these limits requires new assumptions, new tests, and new validation.
