# Phase B.1a — Adversarial Quarantine

## Objective

Phase B.1a tests whether **localized adversarial behavior** can be contained without cascading effects.

The goal was not to prevent adversarial input, but to observe:
- detection,
- isolation,
- and system response.

---

## Adversarial Injection

A single recipient segment was deliberately crafted to:
- exceed entropy thresholds,
- violate expected structural balance,
- and stress diagnostic limits.

All other segments remained unchanged.

---

## Detection Mechanisms

An independent diagnostic pair evaluated:
- structural coherence,
- entropy stress,
- and deviation from expected bounds.

No single diagnostic was considered authoritative.

---

## Observations

### 1. Immediate Detection

The adversarial segment:
- triggered entropy stress alerts,
- failed diagnostic concurrence,
- and was flagged deterministically.

---

### 2. Localized Quarantine

The response was:
- quarantine of the offending segment only,
- no halting of the broadcast,
- no impact on other recipients.

Other segments continued normal operation.

---

### 3. No Cascade Effects

Critically:
- no drift increase was observed in non-adversarial recipients,
- no diagnostic contamination occurred,
- and no shared state was affected.

---

## Failure Mode Behavior

The system:
- failed visibly,
- failed locally,
- and failed reversibly.

No recovery logic was required beyond isolation.

---

## Key Result

Phase B.1a demonstrated that **adversarial behavior can be contained structurally**, not through global shutdown or trust assumptions.

This validated the system’s ability to:
- tolerate faults,
- isolate risk,
- and continue operating safely.

---

## Exit Criteria (Met)

- ✅ Adversarial input detected
- ✅ Quarantine localized
- ✅ No cascade or cross-contamination
- ✅ Diagnostics remained trustworthy

Phase B.1a cleared the protocol to advance to segmented packet experiments (Phase B.2).
